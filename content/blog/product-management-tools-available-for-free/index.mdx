## What can I get for free?

Even at the very beginning of your journey to build a product, using dedicated tools will help you a lot. Since the very beginning, try to apply a process that you can extend and improve later. Your primary duty at that point is planning and the first steps of the execution. Be focused on those activities:

<ul className="list-disc">
  <li>document your goals</li>
  <li>build ideas backlog</li>
  <li>keep a repository of your experiments</li>
  <li>organize backlog for execution phases</li>
  <li>keep product data organized - try not to spread it everywhere around</li>
</ul>

At the very beginning, it is maybe not the smartest idea to spend a lot of money on paid solutions. On the other hand, it would be a nice start with the right foot. Fortunately, many tools have a free plan for people like you. They are limited, but probably going to work for many small product teams.

Below you will find a list which is based on my <Link to="/product-management-tools-popularity/">popularity ranking</Link>. I’ve chosen those with free plans, and I’ll summarize their limitations bellow.
